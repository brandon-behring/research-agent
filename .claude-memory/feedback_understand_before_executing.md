Across the 2026-05-27 session Brandon repeatedly rejected premature execution plans (ExitPlanMode ×3), each time asking to "dig deeper / understand my goals / address inconsistencies / get a broader set of my interests" — never about execution mechanics.

**Why:** he uses planning as a *thinking space* to build shared understanding. He wants his goals (long- AND short-term), the full scope of his work, and his intellectual identity genuinely grasped — and plan inconsistencies explicitly surfaced — before any commitment to execution.

**How to apply:** lead with deep goal discovery + a full-scope read of his machine/projects; explicitly name and resolve plan inconsistencies; prefer lean "actually-used" steps over big execution programs; don't rush to ExitPlanMode or heavy work — converge only once he signals the understanding is right. Complements [[feedback-pacing-one-question]] and [[feedback-epistemic-separation]].

**Refined 2026-05-28 (after ~7 ExitPlanMode rejections across two sessions):** rejection of ExitPlanMode ≠ rejection of the proposed execution path. It almost always means "you haven't surfaced enough yet" — the SAME execution plan often gets approved 2-3 rounds later, after enough inconsistency-surfacing has occurred. The path to acceptance is **thorough inconsistency-surfacing + a question concrete enough to feel meaningful** — NOT converging faster, NOT minimizing planning rounds. Default mode per plan-mode round: name 5-10 inconsistencies (don't pre-filter "minor" ones — surface them all, including assumptions I've been quietly making), then ask 1 substantive question. The conversation IS the value; the plan-file artifact is the byproduct. Related: [[feedback-regression-check-on-regather]] (same epistemic discipline: never silently launder).
