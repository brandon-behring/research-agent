**The eval question for a dossier-driven concept KG = 4 quality layers:**
1. **Extraction** — are the concepts the right ones? (recall: did we miss concepts a human would extract; precision: are extracted ones real, not paper-titles/dupes). Gate: gold set.
2. **Resolution/anchor** — does each concept point to the RIGHT primary source? (anchor correctness). Mostly code + spot-check.
3. **Bridge** — are concept↔concept connections MEANINGFUL, not citation coincidence? (precision via LLM-judge + spot-check; recall via the authored-bridge oracle).
4. **Dedup** — are semantic near-duplicate concepts collapsed? (exact-match dedup left ~some; embeddings power a near-pair check).

**Faithfulness vs truth (epistemic, [[feedback-epistemic-separation]]):** eval must separate "concept faithfully represents the dossier claim" (faithfulness) from "the claim is true of the world" (out of scope — synthesis layer represents what dossiers say, not ground truth).
